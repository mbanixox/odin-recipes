Jollof Rice

Description

This is a foolproof recipe for cooking Jollof rice, West Africaâ€™s most popular dish. I like to serve it as a side with my West African-style grilled beef (Suya), but it can also be a main course.

Ingredients

Tomato Sauce

  • 1 large, ripe tomato, cored and quartered
  • 1 large red bell pepper, seeded and roughly chopped
  • 1 whole habanero pepper, stem removed
  • 1 whole Fresno chili, or jalapeno, stem removed
  • 4 cloves garlic, peeled
  • 1 tablespoon minced ginger
  • 2 cups water

Rice

  • 1/3 cup olive
  • 1 red onion, diced
  • 1 teaspoon salt, plus more to taste
  • 1/4 cup tomato paste
  • 2 tablespoons smoked paprika
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 tablespoon chicken bouillon paste
  • 1/4 cup chopped green onion for garnish (optional)
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro, for garnish (optional)

Directions

Step 1

Combine tomato, red bell pepper, habanero pepper, fresno chili, garlic, ginger, and water in a blender for the sauce. Blend on high until smooth and set aside.

Step 2

Heat oil in a saucepan over medium heat. Add onion and salt and saute until onion starts to soften, 4 to 5 minutes. Clear space in the middle of the pan, add tomato paste, and saute for 2 to 3 minutes. Season with paprika, curry powder, cumin, thyme, pepper, turmeric; continue cooking for 1 to 2 minutes until everything is well combined.

Step 3

Turn off the heat, and stir in the rice, making sure every grain of rice is coated with the oil-tomato mixture. Add bay leaf and chicken bouillon paste.

Step4

Turn heat to high, stir in tomato sauce, and bring to a vigorous simmer. Cover with tight fitting lid, reduce heat to medium-low, and simmer for 20 minutes. Do not remove the lid or stir the rice.

Step 5

Turn off heat after 20 minutes, and set the timer for 12 minutes. Allow the rice to sit and do not remove the lid.

Step 6

After the 12 minutes are up, remover the lid, and fluff the rice with a fork, separating all the grains. Serve garnished with green onions and chopped cilantro.

Cook's Note

If the rice seems to be a little undercooked at this point, stop fluffing. Pat the rice down gently with the back of the fork, cover tightly again, and set heat to medium low. Cook for another 5 minutes, and check again.
